Investigation Report

32 Paid Leads Lost to a reCAPTCHA Bug

Between March 7 and March 24, 2026, the Mira Mar website contact form silently rejected 32 legitimate lead submissions. The visitors filled out the form and thought they submitted it successfully — but Spark never received their data. Here’s what happened, who was affected, and how it was fixed.

Period: Mar 7 – Mar 26, 2026 Prepared: March 26, 2026 Source: CallRail × Spark × Failed Submissions Log
01 — Executive Summary

What Happened

The website contact form posts directly to Spark’s servers. When visitors arrived from social media apps (Facebook, Instagram) or certain browsers, Spark’s reCAPTCHA verification failed — silently blocking the submission. The visitor saw no error. CallRail captured the form data, but Spark never received it.

02 — Root Cause Analysis

Why Spark Rejected These Leads

183 total form submissions were rejected by Spark. After removing 51 spam/bot attempts, 132 were real visitors. The rejection reasons break down into clear categories, all related to reCAPTCHA verification.

🔒

reCAPTCHA Token Missing

The form posted to Spark without a reCAPTCHA token. This happens when the browser blocks the reCAPTCHA script from loading — common in Facebook/Instagram in-app browsers that restrict third-party JavaScript.

05 — Resolution

How This Was Fixed

The root cause was that the website form posted directly to Spark’s servers (cross-origin). This caused browsers to block reCAPTCHA scripts, especially in social media in-app browsers. The fix was deployed on March 24, 2026.

All 32 leads have been recovered

Every affected contact was pushed into Spark via the API with their correct marketing source and UTM tracking data. The form has been fixed to prevent future losses.

Step 1
Server-Side Form Relay

Forms now POST to the WordPress server first, which relays to Spark server-side. This eliminates the cross-origin reCAPTCHA problem entirely.

Step 2
Lead Recovery via API

All 32 missing contacts were pushed to Spark via the API with their original marketing source and UTM campaign data preserved.

Step 3
Monitoring Deployed

UTM cookie diagnostics are now tracking every visitor to confirm the fix is working. Zero cookie failures in the first 24 hours of monitoring.

Important Caveat: Spark Registration Dates

The Spark API does not allow backdating. All recovered contacts show a Spark registration date of March 24, 2026 (the date they were pushed via API), not their original form submission date. The CallRail Date column in the table above reflects the actual date each person submitted the form.
