01 — PrinciplesHow the brand behaves

Heritage, held lightly.

Mira Mar's identity sits at the intersection of 1920s coastal romance and contemporary restraint. Compositions breathe; type carries the weight; color is used sparingly and with intention. Imagery does the storytelling — chrome, ornament and gradients stay out of its way.

Quiet

Generous whitespace. One idea per view. Never more than two type sizes in a single block. Let the page exhale before it speaks.

Ornate where it matters

Reserve the script display face for moments of arrival — hero, section openers, signature. Everywhere else, lean on small caps and Baskerville.

Coastal, not nautical

Colors come from sunset, terracotta and sea-grape. Avoid navy, rope, anchors, palm-tree iconography. The reference is Sarasota in 1922, not a yacht club.

02 — TypographyFour families, in concert

A serif-led hierarchy.

A script display face for emotional peaks, small-caps Caslon for titles and numerals, Baskerville for long-form prose, and Work Sans for utility. Keep loose 7% letter-spacing on caps; keep body line-height generous (1.85–1.9).

03 — ColorFive hues. Held in balance.

A palette of dusk and limestone.

Every color in the system descends from two parents: a deep oxblood drawn from the brand mark and a dusty rose warmed by Florida sunset. Warm cream and paper neutrals carry the rest. Black and pure white are forbidden in body — use ink and paper instead.

04 — MarkLockups & clearspace

A wordmark, not a logo.

The mark is set in spaced small-caps with a hairline rule and an italic descriptor. Always reproduce on a single field — never on photography. Maintain a clearspace equal to the height of the wordmark on all four sides.

PrimaryOxblood mark on cream · preferred
ReverseCream mark on oxblood
EditorialCream mark on rose · sparingly
Secondary mark

A horizontal lockup for navigation, signatures and constrained surfaces. Reproduce in oxblood, cream or rose only — never in arbitrary tints, never on photography without a solid plate.

05 — ActionsButtons, links, fields

Restrained calls to action.

Primary actions are set in spaced small-caps with a hairline border. Italic Baskerville links carry secondary navigation. Avoid pill shapes, drop shadows or rounded corners — squared geometry signals confidence.

06 — FormsHairline inputs, no chrome

Conversation, not bureaucracy.

Inputs are a single hairline below the field. Labels sit above in spaced small-caps. Radio circles match the line weight of the inputs themselves. No background fills, no rounded corners.

Historic timeline

1902

Sarasota is incorporated as a town under the motto, "May Sarasota Prosper."

1910

Chicago socialite and philanthropist Bertha Palmer visits Sarasota, forging a powerful link between Sarasota and Chicago society.

March 1922

The Carman Company receives the largest construction contract in city history to build the Mira Mar Apartments.

December 1922

Ground is broken for the "Half Million Dollar Apartment House," the largest building permit in Sarasota's history at the time.

08 — Hero patternImagery + script display

An entrance, every time.

Hero compositions place a script headline against a darkened photograph — overlay opacity 20–35%. Eyebrow above, italic lede beneath, single CTA. Never crowd a hero with more than one action.

09 — SpacingAn 8-point base

Generous, deliberate.
